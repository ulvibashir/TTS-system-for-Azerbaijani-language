------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.2. Text Normalization and Linguistic Rule Design
------------------------------------------------------------------------

2.2.1. Text Normalization Overview
------------------------------------

Before phoneme-level processing can begin, the raw input text must be converted
from its written form to a fully pronounceable word sequence. Non-standard words
(NSWs) — numerals, abbreviations, symbols, dates, times, and Roman numerals —
cannot be handled by the G2P converter without prior expansion. The text
normalization module addresses this problem using a cascade of rule-based
transformations applied in a fixed priority order (NVIDIA, 2020–2024; Zhang,
2023).

Azerbaijani normalization has two language-specific properties not shared with
most European languages:

  - Azerbaijani numerals follow a compositional structure built from units, tens,
    hundreds, thousands, and millions in most-significant-first order.
  - Ordinal suffixes and case suffixes attached to numerals in writing must be
    selected using vowel harmony with the last vowel of the expanded number word.

text to pronounceable Azerbaijani word sequence]

2.2.2. Normalization of Numbers, Abbreviations, Dates, and Symbols
--------------------------------------------------------------------

The normalization module applies eight processing steps in the order below:

STEP 1 — PUNCTUATION NORMALIZATION
Unicode quotation marks, dashes (—, –, −), ellipses (…), and non-breaking
spaces are converted to normalized ASCII equivalents. This prevents downstream
encoding errors in the G2P pipeline.

STEP 2 — SYMBOL EXPANSION
Currency and unit symbols are replaced with their Azerbaijani spoken forms:
  ₼ → "manat"        $ → "dollar"      € → "avro"
  km → "kilometr"    °C → "dərəcə Selsiy"
  & → "və"           @ → "at"           # → "xəş"

STEP 3 — DATE NORMALIZATION
Dates in DD.MM.YYYY format are converted to spoken form with ordinal suffix
selection governed by vowel harmony:
  Example: 15.06.2024 →
    "on beşinci iyun iki min iyirmi dördüncü il"
  The ordinal suffix (-inci/-üncü/-uncu/-ıncı) is chosen by harmonizing with
  the last vowel of the cardinal number word.

STEP 4 — TIME NORMALIZATION
Times in HH:MM format are expanded to spoken form:
  Example: 09:30 → "doqquz saat otuz dəqiqə"

STEP 5 — ORDINAL NUMBER EXPANSION
Digit strings with hyphen-attached ordinal suffixes are converted to word form
with harmony-correct suffix selection:
  1-ci → "birinci"       3-üncü → "üçüncü"       11-inci → "on birinci"

STEP 6 — CARDINAL NUMBER EXPANSION
Standalone integers are expanded using the Azerbaijani compositional number
system:
  Units (1–9):   bir, iki, üç, dörd, beş, altı, yeddi, səkkiz, doqquz
  Tens (10–90):  on, iyirmi, otuz, qırx, əlli, altmış, yetmiş, səksən, doxsan
  100: yüz    1,000: min    1,000,000: milyon
  Example: 1,947 → "min doqquz yüz qırx yeddi"

STEP 7 — ROMAN NUMERAL EXPANSION
Roman numerals in unambiguous contexts (chapter numbers, century markers) are
converted to their Arabic-digit equivalents and then expanded to words.

STEP 8 — ABBREVIATION EXPANSION
A lookup table maps common Azerbaijani abbreviations to their full spoken forms:
  "AZ" → "Azərbaycan"    "s." → "sahifə"    "il." → "ilə"

The normalization accuracy achieved in evaluation is shown in Table 2.3:

[Table 2.3: Text normalization accuracy by category (100 test sentences)]

| Category           | Correct   | Accuracy |
+--------------------+-----------+----------+

| Ordinal numbers    | 17 / 18   |  94.4%   |
| Dates (DD.MM.YYYY) | 12 / 12   | 100.0%   |
| Times (HH:MM)      | 10 / 10   | 100.0%   |

2.2.3. Syllabification and Stress Assignment Rules
----------------------------------------------------

Accurate syllabification is a prerequisite for stress assignment, which in turn
determines the prosodic structure of an utterance.

SYLLABIFICATION
The syllabifier implements the onset-maximization principle:
  - Every syllable must contain exactly one vowel nucleus.
  - V.CV: a single consonant between two vowels goes to the right onset.
    Example: al.ma (apple), a.ta (father)
  - VC.CV: two consonants between vowels split as coda + onset.
    Example: məkt.əb (school), qalaq → qa.laq
  - VC.CCV: in three-consonant clusters, the first consonant closes the left
    syllable; the remaining form the onset of the right syllable if phonotactically
    permitted.

STRESS ASSIGNMENT
Lexical stress applies a priority-ordered rule hierarchy (Salimi, n.d.):

  Priority 1 — Function words and clitics are marked unstressed.
    Postpositions, conjunctions, and interrogative particles
    (mi, mı, mu, mü) receive no stress regardless of syllabic structure.

  Priority 2 — Lexical exceptions.
    Exception list covers irregularly stressed words:
      deyil (is not) → stress on first syllable (DEY-il)
      hər (every), bir (indefinite) → typically unstressed in phrases.

  Priority 3 — Negation suffix rule.
    Words ending in -ma/-mə (verbal negation) receive stress on the
    penultimate syllable:
      getmədi → GET-mə-di (didn't go)
      gəlməyib → GƏL-mə-yib (hasn't come)

  Priority 4 — Default rule.
    All remaining words receive stress on the final syllable.

PHRASAL STRESS
After lexical stress assignment, phrasal stress demotions apply:
  - Function words within a phrase are demoted.
  - The content word immediately before the main verb receives the nuclear
    pitch accent (most prominent accent in the phrase).
  - Sentence type determines the nuclear accent type (see Section 2.2.4).

2.2.4. Prosodic and Intonation Modeling
-----------------------------------------

The prosody engine models suprasegmental properties using a simplified ToBI
(Tones and Break Indices) framework (Sadekova et al., 2024; Voice synthesis
improvement, 2024). Five sentence types are recognized and associated with
distinct intonation patterns:

[Table 2.4: Sentence types, surface detection markers, and ToBI patterns]
+----------------+-----------------------------+--------+--------+------+
| Sentence Type  | Detection Marker            | Nuc.   | Phrase | BT   |
|                |                             | Accent | Accent | Tone |
+----------------+-----------------------------+--------+--------+------+
| Declarative    | period / no special marker  | H*     | L+H*   | L%   |
| Yes/No Question| mi/mı/mu/mü particle        | L*     | L+H*   | H%   |
| Wh-Question    | wh-word (nə, kim, harada…)  | H*     | L+H*   | L%   |
| Exclamatory    | exclamation mark (!)        | H*+L   | H*     | L%   |
| Imperative     | bare verb form (no tense)   | H*     | —      | L%   |
+----------------+-----------------------------+--------+--------+------+
BT = boundary tone.
[Source: ToBI framework following Sadekova et al. (2024); intonation patterns
derived from Azerbaijani phonetic descriptions in Salimi (n.d.) and Cambridge
Core (n.d.).]

Phone duration is estimated using a rule-based model with the following
baseline values, calibrated to Azerbaijani speech rates (Various authors, 2023):
  - Vowels:    80 ms (unstressed short) to 130 ms (stressed or lengthened)
  - Stops:     60–80 ms
  - Fricatives:80–100 ms
  - Nasals / liquids: 60–70 ms
  - Geminates: 1.6× the corresponding singleton duration
  - Phrase-final lengthening: all phones ×1.3

Pauses are inserted at:
  - Sentence boundary: 500 ms
  - Comma / semicolon boundary: 200 ms
  - Heavy clause boundary (detected by conjunction position): 150 ms

Speaking rate is configurable: neutral = 140 wpm, formal = 120 wpm,
conversational = 160 wpm. Duration estimates are scaled proportionally.
